Investors see hype before they see risk.

Retail investors are surrounded by social media signals, but most tools only show price charts, headlines, or raw discussion threads. HeatScope solves the missing layer: measuring when social attention itself becomes a risk signal.

01

Social attention is fragmented

Discussions are scattered across PTT, Reddit, ticker aliases, company names, and different languages.

02

Heat is not always bullish

A discussion spike may come from a breakthrough, panic, disappointment, or pure speculation.

03

FOMO creates hidden drawdown risk

By the time a stock becomes socially unavoidable, the risk of chasing after a crowded move may already be elevated.

HeatScope converts attention into explainable risk signals.

The product combines social heat, abnormality relative to each stock’s own history, price momentum, volume confirmation, sentiment, and risk explanations into a single heat_score.

Z

Social heat Z-score

Compares current discussion heat against a one-year stock-specific baseline.

P

Price confirmation

Checks whether social heat is accompanied by recent price momentum.

V

Volume anomaly

Detects whether trading participation is rising together with attention.

S

Sentiment matrix

Classifies positive heat, negative heat, panic-style heat, and neutral watch states.

Heat Period Rule

A social high-heat period is triggered when a stock’s social_heat_zscore reaches 2 or above. While active, the period is extended when the Z-score remains above 1.
